Bleeding

Begin by applying a dressing and maintaining direct pressure over the wound.

If an extra hand is needed, ask the patient to hold their own dressing. Start bandaging by laying the roller-gauze at a slight angle.

Pull gently, holding the rolled portion on top to keep it tight. Create an "anchor wrap" by leaving the beginning edge of the gauze as a tab; straighten the bandage after the first pass, then pull the angled edge back.

Then go around again, bandaging over the loose end during the second pass.

Continue wrapping around the arm.

Using an X pattern, crossing over the dressing at an angle each time around, pulling snugly, but not too tightly, until the bandage is about one inch or more beyond both edges of the dressing.

When the wound is covered, pull a long piece of the bandage in front, place a finger across it.

Pull the roll back over your finger to form a sling.

Pull the sling under the extremity to create two ends for tying.

Tie the bandage directly over the wound site to create an additional pressure point.

Tie a knot and secure tightly. Check to make sure there isn't too much room beneath the bandage.

You shouldn't be able to slip a finger under the wrap. Finish by checking CMSTP.
