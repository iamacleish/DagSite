2C-Clash-C

C - Start Manual C Spine

    Start Manual C Spine

  • Call 911 (helper 2).
  • Talk to patient. Instruct them not to move their head.
  • Select person to do manual C-Spine and instruct them without actually touching the subject's head.
  • Helper 1 manually immobilizes patient's head.

C - CMSTP

    Check CMSTP

  • Check hands and feet. Compare hands to hands and feet to feet.
  • Color. Skin color should be healthy.
  • Mobility. Patient should be able to move all fingers and squeeze my fingers.
  • Sensitivity. Should be able to tell which finger I am pinching.
  • Temperature. Should be "normal."
  • Perfusion/Pulse. Pressure finger nail until white. Nail should go pink in 1-2 seconds after release. Check pulse.
  • Maintain rapport with patient.

C - Apply the Collar

    Placing the Cervical Collar on the Patient

  • Measure distance from chin plane to shoulder/neck plane on the patient - use fingers widths.
  • Adjust C-collar to correct extension - to holes completely covered by fingers.
  • Slip collar under subject's neck to see tab.
  • Swing collar around so centerline meets center of chin.
  • Hold collar in place with right hand and push near side in and then pull Velcro tight to snug up collar on both sides. Ensure collar is tight to both sides of neck. Don't jostle head/neck.
  • Ask person holding head (helper 1) if the collar is straight.
  • Helper 1 continues holding head.

L - Lay Subject on Splint (VS)

    Lay Subject on Vacuum Splint

  • Remove Vacuum Splint from container and lay out flat on the ground.
  • Release buckles on side nearest patient.
  • Soften VS if not already soft.
  • Use valve at bottom of VS to soften.
  • Even out filling material in VS. Make sure there is enough filling around the head area.
  • Harden VS until stiff - roughly 15 pumps.
  • Instruct helpers in roll/alignment technique before starting subject alignment. Instruct person holding head on what they do.
  • Move subject arm ontop of their chest
  • Lead Medical (me) grabs shoulders and hip or waist. Grabs pants if convenient.
  • Bottom guy (to my right) overlaps left arm with me and grabs far pant leg.
  • Person holding head calls the roll. Roll patient up on side (toward me) against near helpers legs.
  • Do back inspection while subject is up on side; neck, spine, back, butt, back of legs, and shoulders. Check for bleeding.
  • Far helper pushes VS against subject and at 45 degree angle - check VS is stiff. Head Guy calls rolling subject down/over onto VS with back to ground.

A - Align Subject on VS

    Align the Subject on the Vacuum Splint

  • Bottom helper (to my right) grabs cuff of pants of both legs. Two upper helpers grab shoulder and waist.
  • Botom helper lifts legs one inch off VS during slide to alignment.
  • Slide down 6 inches and in to center of VS. Head guy calls the numbers and starts the slide on three.
  • Helpers continue to hold subject. Slide up 6 inches and align subject to center of VS.
  • Head should not extend above the top of the circle on the Vacuum Splint.
  • OK to move feet into final alignment/center of VS without moving torso.
  • Keep talking to subject

S - Strap Subject into VS

    Strap Patient into Vacuum Splint

  • Soften matt - release valve at bottom of VS.
  • Click in buckles with straps over or under patient arms for comfort.
  • Start at top and snug up straps - push and pull strap so not to pull VS at buckles.
  • Place excess strap at bottom between subjectâ€™s legs then snug up strap again; bottom to top.
  • Use knees to push sides of VS into subject for tight fit. Roll VS back around feet for access during final CMSTP (not now).

H - Secure Subject's Head

    Secure head

  • Helper holding head (head person) continues to hold head.
  • Snug up the Vacuum Splint around the subject's head and hands of the person holding subject's head.
  • Curl back top of VS away from face. Keep secure against cheeks.
  • My hands go over outside of VS at head to secure VS against head.
  • Head person's hands slip out of VS and then over mine to put their hands on the outside of VS around head.
  • Head person holds patient head tight through VS and assure VS is tight against subject's head.
  • Head person keeps holding.
  • Call for 15 pumps to harden VS.
  • Check VS around head to assure the VS does not move away from the patient's head.
  • Check rest of VS for snugness.
  • Replace red cap on valve.
  • Check head support is good.
  • Place tape across head and both sides of VS.

C - CMSTP Again

    Check CMSTP

  • Check hands and feet. Compare hands to hands and feet to feet.
  • Color. Skin color should be healthy.
  • Mobility. Patient should be able to move all fingers and squeeze my fingers.
  • Sensitivity. Should be able to tell which finger I am pinching.
  • Temperature. Should be "normal."
  • Perfusion/Pulse. Pressure finger nail until white. Nail should go pink in 1-2 seconds after release. Check pulse.
  • Maintain rapport with patient.
