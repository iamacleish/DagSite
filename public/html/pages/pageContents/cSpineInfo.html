C-Spine/Packaging

2C-Clash-C

C - Start Manual C Spine

    Start Manual C Spine (person 1)

  • Call 911 (person 2).
  • Talk to patient.
  • Select person to do manual C-Spine and instruct them without actually touching the subject's head.
  • Person 1 manually immobilizes patient's head.

C - CMSTP

    Check CMSTP

  • Check hands and feet.
  • Color. Skin color should be healthy pink.
  • Mobility. Patient should be able to move all fingers and wrists and squeeze my fingers.
  • Sensitivity. Should be able to tell which finger I am pinching.
  • Temperature. Should be "normal."
  • Perfusion. Pressure finger nail until white. Nail should go pink in 1-2 seconds after release
  • Keep Talking to Patient.

C - Apply the Collar

    Placing the Cervical Collar on the Patient

  • Measure distance from chin plane to shoulder/neck plane on the patient – use fingers widths.
  • Adjust C-collar to correct extension - to holes completely covered by fingers.
  • Slip collar under subjects neck to see tab.
  • Swing collar around so centerline meets center of chin.
  • Hold collar in place with right hand and push near side in and then pull Velcro tight to snug up collar on both sides. Check if near side is pulled in enough and far side curves around. Don't jostle head/neck.
  • Ask person holding head (person 1) if the collar is straight.
  • Person 1 continues holding head.

L - Lay out Vacuum Splint (VS)

    Lay Out the Vacuum Splint

  • Remove Vacuum Splint from container and lay out flat on the ground.
  • Release buckles on side nearest patient.
  • Inflate VS if not already inflated.
  • Use valve at bottom of VS to inflate VS.
  • Replace red valve cap when done.
  • Even out filling material in VS. Make sure there is enough filling around the head area.
  • Deflate until stiff - maybe 15 pumps.

A - Align Subject on VS

    Align the Subject on the Vacuum Splint

  • Instruct helpers in roll/alignment technique before starting subject alignment. Instruct person holding head on what he does.
  • Move subject arm to sort of above head - optional.
  • Top near guy (me) grabs shoulders and hip or waist. Grabs pants if convenient.
  • Bottom guy (to my right) overlaps left arm with me and grabs far pant leg.
  • Person holding head calls the roll. Roll patient up on side (toward me) against near helpers legs.
  • Do back inspection while subject is up on side; spine, butt, legs, and shoulders. Check for bleeding.
  • Far helper pushes VS against subject and at 45 degree angle - check VS is stiff. Roll subject down/over onto VS with back to ground.
  • Align subject on Vacuum Splint. Bottom helper (to my right) grabs inner part of pants of both legs. Two upper helpers grab shoulder and waist. Slide down 6 inches and in to alignment. Head guy calls the numbers and starts the slide on three.
  • Then the same but slide up and align. Head should be in the circle on the Vacuum Splint OK to move feet into final alignment.
  • Keep talking to subject

S - Strap Subject into VS

    Strap Patient into Vacuum Splint

  • Inflate matt - release valve at bottom of VS.
  • Click in buckles with straps over patient.
  • Start at top and snug up belt - push and pull belt so not to pull VS at buckles.
  • Place excess belt at bottom between subject’s legs then snug up belt again bottom to top.
  • Use knees to push sides of VS into subject for tight fit. Roll VS back around feet for access during final CMSTP (not now).

H - Secure Subject's Head

    Secure head

  • Helper holding head continues to hold head.
  • Snug up the Vacuum Splint around the head and hands of the head guy.
  • Roll back top of VS but keep secure against head on face at about cheek level.
  • My hands go over outside of VS at head to secure VS against head.
  • Head guys hands slip out of VS and then over mine to put him on outside of VS around head.
  • He holds patient head tight through VS and I do final snug up of VS on subjects head.
  • Head guy keeps holding.
  • Call for 15 pumps to harden VS.
  • Check VS around head to assure the VS does not move away from the patient's head.
  • Check rest of VS for snugness.
  • Replace red cap on valve.
  • Check head support is good.
  • Tape up VS over patient head. 18+ inches of tape and crimp in the middle. Tape head and both sides to the VS.

C - CMSTP Again

    Check CMSTP

  • Color, Motion, Sensitivity, Temperature, and Perfusion.
  • Check both hands and feet.
