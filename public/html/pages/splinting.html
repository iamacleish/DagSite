Splinting

Using triangular bandages, create three cravats. Fold the pointed end...

...in towards the long end (top). Fold again (middle) and again (lower).

Roll up some gauze or cloth and put it into the patient's hand. Place the arm into the splint such that the entire portion from elbow to hand is supported. Drape two cravats over the top with one above and one below the break site.

Wrap each bandage around the splint snugly, and tie a knot on the outside of the splint.

Check to make sure the joints are properly immobilized and provide ample padding between the arm and splint.

Take a third triangular bandage and tie a knot at the 90 degree corner. The knot will serve as a "pocket" in which to place the elbow.

Drape the bandage under the splint and across the shoulder opposite the pocket; lift the front portion of the bandage over the splint to the same shoulder as the pocket/elbow.

Tie off to the side of the back of the neck and pad with gauze or cloth to keep the knot from causing friction on the neck.

Check to be sure the sling is secure and keeps the arm elevated.

Use the last cravat to form a swath, wrapping across the chest, from outside of the elbow and under the opposite arm.

Tie off in the back to the side.

After the sling is secure, finish by checking CMSTP again.
